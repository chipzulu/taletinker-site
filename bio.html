Bio

Christian P. Koch

Born in 1962, Christian P. Koch is a writer who straddles the line between memory and fiction, weaving lived experience with an acute sense of presence. His work is a testament to his instinct for storytelling, where every detail—whether a fragment of childhood nostalgia, a moment of high-seas peril, or the quiet hum of a spaceship cockpit—feels immediate, visceral, and undeniably real.

He has spent decades translating complex ideas into precise language that enables safe operation of machines and systems, a skill that now sharpens his fiction to an edge of relentless clarity. He writes not to escape reality but to report straight from inside it, whether that reality is a storm-battered tall ship, a hikers' refuge in the Pyrenees, or the farthest reaches of deep space. His prose carries the weight of truth, even in fiction, and his characters—outsiders, seekers, those who press forward despite uncertainty—move with the same determined pulse that drives his own journey.

His stories align his perception of the world with his heartbeat.
